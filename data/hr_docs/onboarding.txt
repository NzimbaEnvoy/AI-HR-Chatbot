Employee Onboarding Procedure

Overview
The onboarding procedure outlines the structured process followed when a new employee joins the company. The goal of onboarding is to ensure proper integration, role clarity, compliance training, and performance alignment.

Step 1: Probation Period
New employees undergo a 3-month probation period unless otherwise specified in their employment contract. During probation, performance, conduct, and role suitability are evaluated.

Step 2: Equipment Allocation
IT equipment required for the role is issued during onboarding. This may include a laptop, system access credentials, and other necessary tools. Employees must comply with IT security policies.

Step 3: Induction and Mandatory Training
New employees must complete mandatory induction training within the first month of employment. Induction covers company policies, code of conduct, security requirements, and role-specific compliance training.

Step 4: Performance Check-ins
Managers conduct regular check-ins during probation, typically on a monthly basis. These sessions review progress, clarify expectations, and provide feedback and support.

Step 5: Confirmation of Employment
At the end of the probation period, performance is formally reviewed. Successful completion of probation results in confirmation of permanent employment status.

Support During Onboarding
Employees may contact HR or their direct manager for assistance during the onboarding process.
